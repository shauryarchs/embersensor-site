Why EmberSensor exists

Wildfires can spread quickly, and ember-driven ignition can threaten homes before people have enough time to react. EmberSensor was created to bring local sensing, weather awareness, wildfire hotspot data, live monitoring, and automated response together into one connected system.

What the system already does

EmberSensor is already more than an idea. A working prototype has been built with several real capabilities that can be demonstrated today.

Real-time local sensing

Tracks temperature, smoke, and flame-related conditions around the home environment.

Weather-aware risk analysis

Uses live weather conditions such as heat, humidity, wind, and rain to better understand risk.

Nearby wildfire awareness

Integrates satellite-based wildfire hotspot data to detect active fire activity near the surrounding area.

Integrated fire risk scoring

Combines local sensors, weather, and nearby fire activity into a single live fire risk level.

Automated sprinkler response

Supports smart sprinkler activation when dangerous conditions are detected.

Mobile and live monitoring

Includes live status access, map-based fire awareness, and connected camera visibility.
